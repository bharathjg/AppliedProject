About This Project

This project was inspired by the prominence of generative AI in human lives. Generative AI finds applications across multiple domains, improving workflows for the most part. Its capabilities are manifold and is not just a tool for automating functions but is transforming the way information is accessed.


Large Language Models produce responses to queries that can be superior to traditional searching, providing summaries of multiple sources of information that they have been trained on. While this saves time, the caveats of this are that the validity and correctness of the information generated are at question. LLMs are prone to hallucination, a phenomenon where they make up information or produce incorrect information with confidence. The onus of verifying this lies on us, the users.


As generative AI grows to become the norm more and more every day, it is important to understand the issues that come with it and recognize its limitations. AI may automate a lot of things, but it cannot replace human creativity or intuition. Therefore, integrating generative AI into modern curriculums for students of all grade levels is essential.


This website offers 3 exercises built on top of ACRL Information Literacy standards, designed to teach students how to use generative AI tools for looking up information. The exercises involve interactive chat interfaces similar to those which you might find in most LLM based tools and products.


This website was built by Bharat Jayprakash, advised by Dr. Brian Atkinson. Research on integrating generative AI into school curriculums is one of Dr. Atkinson's interests currently and this website serves as an implementation of the recommendations made as part of his research.
